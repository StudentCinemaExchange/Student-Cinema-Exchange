How the exchange model works

+

The platform serves classroom teaching and student development at the same time: faculty gain curated peer films for lessons, and students gain a live library of work to study and learn from.

+
+
+

1) Submit top films

+

Universities and festivals nominate standout student films with rights clearance and release documentation.

+
+
+

2) Screen in class

+

Teachers and students watch other schools' films in classrooms and course contexts under educational use terms.

+
+
+

3) Share view revenue

+

Every paid view is split 50/50, creating direct earnings for student filmmakers and a sustainable platform model.

Billing guardrails

+
    +
  • Monthly floor: $25 minimum invoice per institution.
    • +
  • Monthly ceiling: institution sets the cap (for example $100–$500).
    • +
  • Daily per-film limit: department sets the daily view limit to prevent abuse.
    • +
  • Hard lock: streaming pauses when the monthly cap is reached (unless a top-up pack is purchased).
    • +
  • Free own-content access: institutions watch their own films at no cost.
