How the exchange model works

Film schools, communication departments, and campus festivals can contribute standout student work and gain access to a growing catalog for teaching and peer analysis.

1) Nominate standout films

Departments and partner festivals submit top student films. We maintain rights and release documentation before films are made available.

2) Screen in class

Universities watch other schools’ films through a simple per-view model designed for educational use, discussion, and coursework.

3) Pay students directly

Half of each paid view is reserved for student filmmakers, turning academic screenings into a repeatable income stream.

Simple pricing with guaranteed budget control

No surprise invoices: every school has a minimum spend for participation and a hard maximum to protect annual departmental budgets.

Optional top-up packs (for example, 10 views for $18) can be added after a school reaches the monthly cap.

Billing guardrails

  • Monthly floor: $50 minimum invoice (up to 25 standard views).
  • Monthly cap: $200 maximum invoice (up to 100 standard views).
  • Hard lock: Streaming pauses at 100 views unless a top-up pack is purchased.
  • Free own-content access: Schools never pay to watch their own students’ films.

Pilot workflow: launch in weeks, not months

Week 1: Partner intake

Sign 2–3 pilot festivals or departments, gather top 5–10 films each, and collect release + rights documents.

Week 2: Library setup

Upload films, set access controls, assign metadata, and prepare a curated archive page for institutional viewers.

Week 3+: Department rollout

Enable department access, track screenings, issue monthly invoices, and pay students on a quarterly schedule.
